# Recruiting 15–20 STR operator interviews

## Who qualifies

- Manages **3+ STR properties** (not single spare room)
- **Hot tub** listed as amenity on at least some units
- Roles: operator, co-host, property manager, cleaning lead with tub SOP

## Quota math

| Stage | Count | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Identified leads | 60–80 | ~4× needed completions |
| Outreach sent | 40–50 | Mix channels |
| Replies | 15–25 | ~30–40% reply on warm channels |
| **Completed interviews** | **15–20** | Use [`interview-script.md`](../../docs/validation/interview-script.md) |
| `pilot_yes` signal | **≥5** | Go threshold |

## Where to find them (actionable)

### 1. LinkedIn Sales Navigator or search (free tier works)

Search strings:
- `"vacation rental" "property manager" [your metro]`
- `"short term rental" owner portfolio`
- `"Airbnb co-host" hot tub` (in posts)

Filter: 2nd-degree connections first. Send connection note → email if accepted.

### 2. Facebook groups

Search: `"[City] Airbnb hosts"`, `"STR hosts [state]"`, `"vacation rental hosts"`

Lurk 1 week; answer questions; then post research ask (see marketing-reference).

### 3. BiggerPockets

Forum: Short-Term Rentals & Vacation Rental Properties. Reply to hot tub / turnover threads; DM only if they engaged publicly.

### 4. Local meetups

Meetup.com / Eventbrite: “short term rental”, “Airbnb host”. Attend with printed QR to Calendly (15-min research call).

### 5. Property management companies

Google: `vacation rental management [city] hot tub`. Call front desk; ask for operations manager. Higher quality than single hosts.

### 6. Hot tub dealers / service techs

Ask: “Which STR clients have you serviced?” Request warm intro (best conversion).

### 7. Public registries (where legal)

Some cities publish STR permit lists (business name → website → contact). Use for **identification**, outreach via **business email only**.

### 8. Listing research (identify, do not spam on-platform)

Use Airbnb/Vrbo search filters: **Hot tub** + your target city → note **property management brand** in listing (many portfolios use same logo/name) → find operator website/LinkedIn.

**Never** message the host through Airbnb as a “guest” or misuse reporting contact forms.

## Can you email/DM from their Airbnb listing?

| Method | Verdict |
|--------|---------|
| Airbnb guest message | **No** — ToS violation, unethical, low trust |
| “Contact host” pre-booking for sales | **No** |
| Scraping emails from listing HTML | **No** — often ToS + GDPR/CCPA risk |
| Finding same host on LinkedIn/company site | **Yes** — professional context |
| Business email on PM company website | **Yes** — best channel |
| Facebook group public post asking for calls | **Yes** — opt-in |

## Weekly outreach rhythm (solo founder)

| Day | Activity | Volume |
|-----|----------|--------|
| Mon | LinkedIn connect + note | 10 |
| Tue | FB group value comment + 1 ask post | 3 comments |
| Wed | Follow-up emails (day 5+) | 10 |
| Thu | BiggerPockets / forum | 2 replies |
| Fri | Booked interviews only | — |

## Interview scheduling

- Tool: Calendly 30-min “STR hot tub research”
- Incentive (optional): $25 gift card or free concierge week summary — disclose in invite
- Record: notes only unless consent for recording

## After each interview

1. Append row to `interview-log.csv`
2. Tag signals: `workaround`, `pilot_yes`, `pain_high`, `referral`
3. Weekly roll-up: count toward go/no-go

## Decision tree

```
15+ interviews done?
  ├─ No → keep outreach 2 more weeks; add dealer intros
  └─ Yes → count pilot_yes + pain_high
        ├─ ≥5 → GO: concierge pilot (2–3 operators)
        ├─ 3–4 → NARROW: one metro or PM-only segment
        └─ <3 → PIVOT or SHUTDOWN (document learnings in docs/marketing/)
```
